Diabates

Diabetes is a chronic condition where the body can't regulate blood sugar. It can lead to serious complications but can be managed with diet, exercise, and medication.Diabetes occurs in four stages: Insulin resistance, prediabetes, type 2 diabetes, and type 2 diabetes with vascular complications.

Common Symptoms : Increased thirst, frequent urination, hunger, fatigue and blurred vision. In some cases, there may be no symptoms.

Treatments : Treatments include diet, exercise, medication and insulin therapy.

Liver disease

Any condition that damages the liver and prevents it from functioning well. It's essential to consult a doctor and undergo tests for a better understanding of the underlying cause.

Common Symptoms : include fatigue, weakness, loss of appetite, nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain and swelling, yellowing of the skin and eyes (jaundice), dark urine, pale stools, and itchy skin.

Treatments : Treatments include maintaing a healthy diet and regular exercise, medications to manage symptoms or slow the progression of the disease, and in some cases, surgery or liver transplantation.

Heart disease

(also called:Cardiovascular disease)

Heart conditions that include diseased vessels, structural problems and blood clots. It includes various diseases like Coronary artery disease,Stroke,Arrhythmia etc.

Common Symptoms : include chest pain, discomfort, or pressure (angina), shortness of breath, fatigue, weakness, dizziness, lightheadedness, nausea, sweating, and irregular heartbeat (arrhythmia).

Treatments : Treatment for heart disease can include lifestyle changes, medications like blood thinners and cholesterol-lowering drugs, and procedures such as angioplasty or bypass surgery to improve blood flow.

Kidney Failure

Kidney disease causes a gradual decline in kidney function, leading to a buildup of waste products and fluids, affecting blood filtration and electrolyte balance.

Common Symptoms : include fatigue, weakness, swelling (edema) in the legs, ankles, or around the eyes, foamy or bubbly urine, increased need to urinate, especially at night, and blood in the urine.

Treatments : include medications to control blood pressure, manage blood sugar levels, or treat infections. In advanced cases, dialysis or kidney transplant may be necessary. Treatment plans are tailored to individual needs and should be discussed with a healthcare provider.
